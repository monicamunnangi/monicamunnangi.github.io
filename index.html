Monica Munnangi


🚀 On the Job Market (Summer 2026): Research Scientist | Applied Scientist | ML Scientist ▾

I evaluate and build LLMs that work reliably in the real world. My research focuses on making foundation models trustworthy enough to deploy in high-stakes settings like healthcare and beyond.

Track Record:

  • Published several papers as a lead author on LLM evaluation, safety & reliability (NAACL, ACL, MLHC)
  • Trained & evaluated 70B models across multi-GPU clusters (DeepSpeed, PyTorch)
  • Deployed production clinical AI at GE Healthcare
  • Built FactEHR: framework for evaluating LLM factuality in healthcare

What I Do:
Train foundation models | Design evaluation benchmarks | Fine-tune for specialized domains | Build RAG systems | Ship production ML | Factuality and hallucinations

Looking for roles where AI reliability matters: LLM training, evaluation infrastructure, AI safety, clinical AI, post-training and evaluating LLMs, designing benchmarks, or any position building foundation models people can trust. If there's a good fit, please get in touch—would love to chat.

I am a PhD candidate at Khoury College of Computer Science advised by Prof. Saiph Savage and a member of the Civic A.I. Lab. I interned at Stanford School of Medicine, Shah Lab advised by Jason Fries. I had a fun summer '23 interning at Allen Institute for AI advised by Aakanksha Naik.

My current research interests are generally around improving the performance of LLMs: how they use information, and how to evaluate their performance. I also work on the improving the safety, reliability, and real-world performance of large language models in high-stakes settings. I develop alignment methods through instruction tuning, domain-specific pretraining, supervised fine-tuning, and multi-turn evaluation. While much of my work is grounded in healthcare applications, the approaches I develop generalize to broader domains where trustworthy AI is essential.

Prior to PhD, I graduated from UMass Amherst, with a Masters in Computer Science. During this time, I interned at GE Healthcare as as Data Scientist where I worked on computer vision for medical imaging. I've done my undergrad at Vellore Institute of Technology, Chennai.
